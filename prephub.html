PREPHub Project Work

PREPHub (prephub.org) is a disaster preparedness infrastructure project for urban areas out of the Urban Risk Lab at MIT. I worked as a UROP (undergraduate researcher) to design the touchscreen interface using the Adobe Creative Cloud suite.

Home screen of the touchscreen interface.

Information and a video about the PREPHub project.

This screen shows users information about the PREPHub's sources of energy. One graph shows the output from the built-in solar panels, and the other displays the power from the pedal generators, which allow for emergency and community charging for their portable devices.

This feature allows users to post community news or read important notices from officials during an emergency.

A screen showing the emergency supplies available at each location.

Information about the beacon lights, which can signal an emergency or other important announcements depending on the situation.

Radio playing capabilities to access entertainment for everyday use and news during emergencies.

The camera screen allows the public to take selfies to post on social media, simultaneously raising awareness of the project. During an emergency, citizens can use the camera to check in with loved ones.
